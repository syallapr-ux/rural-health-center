Community Health Education

Preventive Healthcare, Awareness & Capacity Building for Rural Populations

Health education is a core pillar of the National Health Mission (NHM). This module delivers structured, accessible and culturally relevant health information to rural communities, empowering individuals to make informed decisions.

Content is curated based on MoHFW, ICMR and WHO guidelines and is designed for low-literacy and low-connectivity environments.

Maternal & Child Health

Safe motherhood and early childhood survival education.

  • Antenatal care (minimum 4 ANC visits)
  • Institutional delivery importance
  • Nutrition during pregnancy
  • Danger signs during pregnancy & post-partum
  • Breastfeeding and infant nutrition
Program linkage: Janani Suraksha Yojana (JSY), PMMVY

Non-Communicable Diseases (NCDs)

Early detection and lifestyle modification education.

  • Hypertension & diabetes awareness
  • Dietary salt & sugar reduction
  • Physical activity recommendations
  • Tobacco & alcohol risk counselling
Screening supported: NPCDCS Program

Communicable Diseases

Prevention, early reporting and treatment compliance.

  • Tuberculosis symptoms & DOTS adherence
  • Malaria & dengue prevention
  • Water-borne disease prevention
  • Hygiene & sanitation practices
Linked missions: NTEP, NVBDCP, Swachh Bharat

Adolescent Health

Life-skills education and preventive guidance.

  • Menstrual hygiene management
  • Anemia prevention
  • Mental health awareness
  • Substance abuse prevention
Program: Rashtriya Kishor Swasthya Karyakram (RKSK)

Digital Education Delivery

This module is designed to support:

  • Offline-first access via PWA caching
  • Multilingual expansion (regional languages)
  • Audio-visual learning for low-literacy users
  • Future API integration with health content repositories

Content delivery can be dynamically personalized based on age group, gender and risk profile using ABDM-linked metadata.
