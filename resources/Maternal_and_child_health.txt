Maternal and Child Health (MCH) focuses on the physical, mental, and social well-being of women during pregnancy, childbirth, and the postpartum period, as well as the health of infants and children under five. For 2026, healthcare modules prioritize a "life-course approach" that integrates services across different stages of life to improve survival and long-term health outcomes.
1. Core Components of Maternal Care
Comprehensive maternal care is delivered through a continuum of three critical phases:
Antenatal Care (ANC): Regular monitoring during pregnancy to prevent complications.
Registration: Early registration (ideally within the first trimester) to establish baseline health.
Minimum Visits: WHO recommends at least eight antenatal contacts.
Services: Blood pressure monitoring, hemoglobin (Hb) estimation, screening for gestational diabetes, HIV/Syphilis testing, and iron-folic acid (IFA) supplementation.
Intranatal Care: Care during labor and delivery to ensure a safe birth.
Skilled Attendance: Delivery supervised by trained professionals (Skilled Birth Attendants).
Clean Practices: Adhering to the "five cleans" (clean hands, surface, cord cutting, cord tie, and cord care) to prevent infection.
Postnatal Care (PNC): Care for the mother and newborn during the first six weeks after birth.

2. Essential Child Health Services
Child health interventions for 2026 focus on ending preventable deaths and ensuring children are developmentally "on-track".
Newborn Care: Immediate assessment, skin-to-skin contact, thermal protection, and early initiation of breastfeeding (within one hour).
Immunization: Routine vaccinations according to national schedules (e.g., BCG, Hepatitis B).
Nutrition: Promotion of exclusive breastfeeding for the first six months and management of malnutrition.
Growth Monitoring: Systematic tracking of height and weight to detect stunting or wasting.
3. Key Health Indicators for 2026
Success in MCH is measured against global targets, particularly Sustainable Development Goal 3:
Maternal Mortality Ratio (MMR): Aiming for less than 70 deaths per 100,000 live births globally by 2030.
Neonatal Mortality Rate: Reducing to at least as low as 12 per 1,000 live births in every country.
Under-Five Mortality Rate: Targeting at least as low as 25 per 1,000 live births.
4. 2026 Strategic Priorities
Current strategies emphasize several cross-cutting themes:
Digital Health: Using mobile apps and telemedicine to expand access in remote areas.
Respectful Care: Moving beyond clinical safety to prioritize "respectful maternity care" that honors a woman's dignity and autonomy.
Adolescent Health: Including adolescence as a distinct life stage to prevent early pregnancy complications.
Integrated RMNCAH+N: Linking Reproductive, Maternal, Newborn, Child, and Adolescent health with Nutrition for a holistic care pathway.
